1	Carbon dioxide from the air passes through small pores (holes) in the leaves These pores are called stomata.
2	Water is absorbed by the roots and passes through vessels in the stem on its way to the leaves.
3	Chlorophyll absorbs the sun's energy.
4	It is this energy that is used to split water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen.
5	Oxygen is released from the leaves into the atmosphere.
6	Hydrogen and carbon dioxide are used to form glucose or food for plants.
